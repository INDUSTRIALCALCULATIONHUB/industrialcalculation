Air Density

Air density is a fundamental thermophysical property defined as mass per unit volume of air. It plays a critical role in engineering design calculations involving fluid flow, heat transfer, combustion systems, and air handling equipment.

Theory

Air density (ρ) is governed by pressure, temperature, and altitude. It directly affects performance of fans, blowers and thermal systems.

  • ρ = Mass / Volume
  • Unit: kg/m³
  • Standard value: 1.225 kg/m³ (15°C, 1 atm)
  • ρ = P / (R × T)

Key Factors

  • Temperature ↑ → Density ↓
  • Pressure ↑ → Density ↑
  • Altitude ↑ → Density ↓
  • Humidity ↑ → Density ↓

Engineering Relevance

  • Fan performance (ID, FD, Axial, Centrifugal)
  • Duct airflow and pressure calculations
  • Combustion air requirement
  • APC systems (ESP, Bag Filter, Scrubber)
  • Cooling tower air handling

Engineering Note

  • Flow ≈ constant
  • Pressure ∝ Density
  • Power ∝ Density

Always apply density correction for accurate fan and system design.
