Air Density Explained

Air density is the mass of air per unit volume. It is an important property in engineering calculations involving fluid flow, heat transfer, HVAC systems, and aerodynamics.

What is Air Density?

Air density (ρ) is defined as mass per unit volume.

Density = Mass / Volume

Standard Air Density

  • Temperature: 15°C
  • Pressure: 1 atm
  • Air Density ≈ 1.225 kg/m³

Air Density Formula

ρ = P / (R × T)

  • P = Pressure (Pa)
  • R = 287 J/kg·K
  • T = Temperature (K)

Factors Affecting Air Density

  • Temperature increases → density decreases
  • Pressure increases → density increases
  • Altitude increases → density decreases

Engineering Applications

  • HVAC design
  • Ventilation systems
  • Aerodynamics
  • Fluid flow calculations
