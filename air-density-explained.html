Air Density

Air density is defined as mass per unit volume of air and is a critical parameter in fluid flow, thermal systems, and air handling equipment design. It directly influences performance of fans, blowers, combustion systems, and HVAC applications.

Fundamentals

  • ρ = Mass / Volume
  • Unit: kg/m³
  • Standard density: 1.225 kg/m³ (15°C, 1 atm)

Equation

  • ρ = P / (R × T)
  • P = Pressure (Pa)
  • R = 287 J/kg·K
  • T = Temperature (K)

Influencing Parameters

  • Temperature ↑ → Density ↓
  • Pressure ↑ → Density ↑
  • Altitude ↑ → Density ↓
  • Humidity ↑ → Density ↓

Engineering Applications

  • Fan design (ID Fan, FD Fan, PA Fan, Axial & Centrifugal)
  • Duct airflow and pressure calculations
  • HVAC system design and load estimation
  • Cooling tower air handling performance
  • Combustion air and boiler efficiency calculations
  • Air pollution control systems (ESP, Bag Filter, Scrubber)

Engineering Note

  • Flow ≈ constant with density variation
  • Static pressure ∝ air density
  • Power consumption ∝ air density

Fan and blower performance curves are based on standard air conditions. Density correction is essential for accurate equipment selection and system design.
