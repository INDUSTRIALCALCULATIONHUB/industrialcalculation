Hopper Valley Angle

Hopper Valley Angle, Plate Angle, Surface Area & Volume

Engineering Knowledge

A hopper is a structural funnel-shaped component used in bulk material handling systems to control discharge flow. The geometry of a hopper directly affects flow efficiency, material blockage (arching), and structural load distribution.

Geometric Basis of Hopper Design

  • Hopper is modeled as a truncated pyramid (frustum) in engineering calculations
  • All angles are derived from height and planar dimensional differences
  • ΔL and ΔW define convergence from top opening to bottom outlet
  • Valley angle governs material flow behavior at the intersection edges

Flow Engineering Importance

  • Steeper hopper angles reduce material stagnation and bridging
  • Shallow angles may cause arch formation and flow obstruction
  • Uniform wall inclination improves mass flow characteristics
  • Critical angle depends on material friction and bulk density

Structural & Design Considerations

  • Load increases toward hopper bottom due to material column weight
  • Welding joints must resist shear stress at valley intersections
  • Sheet thickness selection depends on material abrasiveness
  • Design must consider dynamic load during discharge

Standard Engineering Formulas

  • ΔL = (Lt − Lb) / 2
  • ΔW = (Wt − Wb) / 2
  • θL = tan⁻¹ ( H / ΔL )
  • θW = tan⁻¹ ( H / ΔW )
  • θV = tan⁻¹ ( H / √(ΔL² + ΔW²) )
  • Volume = (H / 3) × (Atop + Abottom + √(AtopAbottom))

Industrial Applications

  • Cement silos and batching plants
  • Coal and ash handling systems
  • Food grain storage and discharge systems
  • Mining and mineral processing plants
  • Bulk solid material transport systems
