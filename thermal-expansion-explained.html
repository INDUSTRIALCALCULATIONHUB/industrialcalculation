Thermal Expansion Explained

Complete Guide for Engineering Applications

Introduction

Thermal expansion is the tendency of materials to change their dimensions when exposed to temperature variations. It is a fundamental concept in mechanical, civil, and industrial engineering.

Thermal Expansion Formula

The standard linear thermal expansion formula is:

ΔL = α × L × ΔT

  • ΔL = Change in length
  • α = Coefficient of thermal expansion (per °C)
  • L = Original length
  • ΔT = Temperature change

Types of Thermal Expansion

1. Linear Expansion

Change in length of a material due to temperature change. Common in rods, pipes, and beams.

2. Area Expansion

Change in surface area, typically observed in plates and sheets.

3. Volume Expansion

Change in volume, important for fluids and gases.

Coefficient of Thermal Expansion

Each material expands differently. Metals generally expand more than ceramics.

  • Aluminium: High expansion
  • Steel: Moderate expansion
  • Cast Iron: Low expansion

Engineering Applications

  • Piping systems (expansion loops)
  • Bridge expansion joints
  • Railway tracks
  • Machinery components
  • Heat exchangers

Why Thermal Expansion is Important

If thermal expansion is not considered, it can cause:

  • Structural failure
  • Cracks and deformation
  • Equipment misalignment

Example Calculation

A steel rod of length 1000 mm is heated by 50°C.

If α = 12 × 10⁻⁶ /°C:

ΔL = 12×10⁻⁶ × 1000 × 50 = 0.6 mm
