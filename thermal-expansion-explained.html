Thermal Expansion Explained

Understanding material expansion in engineering

What is Thermal Expansion?

Thermal expansion is the increase in size of a material when temperature rises. This effect must be considered in engineering design to avoid structural failure.

Formula

ΔL = α × L × ΔT

  • ΔL = Change in length
  • α = Coefficient of expansion
  • L = Original length
  • ΔT = Temperature change

Why It Matters

  • Prevents structural cracks
  • Important in piping systems
  • Used in bridge and building design

Applications

  • Mechanical engineering
  • Civil structures
  • Heat exchangers
  • Industrial equipment
