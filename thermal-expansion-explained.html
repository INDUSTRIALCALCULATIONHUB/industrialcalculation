Thermal Expansion Explained

Engineering behavior of materials under heat

What is Thermal Expansion?

Thermal expansion is the tendency of materials to increase in size when exposed to heat. This effect is critical in engineering design to prevent structural damage.

Formula

ΔL = α × L × ΔT

  • ΔL = Change in length
  • α = Coefficient of thermal expansion
  • L = Original length
  • ΔT = Temperature change

Engineering Importance

  • Prevents pipe stress and failure
  • Critical in bridge expansion joints
  • Used in mechanical system design

Applications

  • Piping systems in power plants
  • Heat exchangers
  • Structural steel design
  • Railway track expansion control

Theory

Thermal expansion occurs due to increased atomic vibration when temperature rises, causing materials to expand.

Information

  • Expansion depends on material type
  • Metals expand more than concrete
  • High temperatures increase structural stress

Notes

  • Always consider expansion gaps in design
  • Use correct coefficient values for accuracy
