Heat Transfer Explained

Understanding Conduction, Convection & Radiation

📌 Introduction

Heat transfer is the movement of thermal energy from a hotter object to a colder one. It is a fundamental concept in thermodynamics and plays a critical role in engineering systems.

🔥 Modes of Heat Transfer

1. Conduction

Heat transfer through direct contact of molecules in solids. Example: Heat flow through a metal rod.

Fourier’s Law: Q = kA(dT/dx)

2. Convection

Heat transfer through fluid motion (liquids and gases). Example: Boiling water circulation.

Newton’s Law of Cooling: Q = hA(Ts - T∞)

3. Radiation

Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves without needing a medium. Example: Heat from the sun.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law: Q = σεAT⁴

🏭 Engineering Applications

  • Boilers and heat exchangers
  • Power plants
  • HVAC systems
  • Industrial cooling systems
