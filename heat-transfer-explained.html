Heat Transfer Explained

Understanding Conduction, Convection & Radiation in Engineering Systems

Introduction

Heat transfer is the movement of thermal energy from a higher temperature region to a lower temperature region. It is a fundamental concept in thermodynamics and is widely used in mechanical, chemical, and thermal engineering.

Modes of Heat Transfer

1. Conduction

Conduction is heat transfer through direct molecular interaction in solids. Example: Heat flow through a metal rod or wall.

Fourier’s Law: Q = kA(dT/dx)

2. Convection

Convection is heat transfer through fluid motion (liquids and gases). Example: Boiling water or air circulation in HVAC systems.

Newton’s Law of Cooling: Q = hA(Ts - T∞)

3. Radiation

Radiation is heat transfer through electromagnetic waves without requiring a medium. Example: Solar heat transfer to Earth.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law: Q = σεAT⁴

Engineering Applications

  • Heat exchangers in power plants
  • Boilers and thermal systems
  • HVAC and refrigeration systems
  • Industrial process cooling
