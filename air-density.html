Enter altitude in meters and temperature in °C

Theory

Air density is mass per unit volume of air and varies with temperature and pressure.

Information

  • Higher temperature reduces density
  • Higher pressure increases density
  • HVAC system design (airflow, duct sizing, load calculation)
  • Fan and blower selection (CFM, static pressure, performance correction)
  • Duct and chimney draft calculations
  • Aerodynamic analysis (drag and lift estimation)
  • Air pollution control systems (ESP, bag filter, scrubber sizing)
  • Combustion air requirement calculations
  • Process engineering and fluid flow analysis

Notes

  • Standard density at sea level: 1.225 kg/m³
  • Based on ideal gas law
  • Valid for standard atmosphere (0–11 km altitude)

Formula

  • ρ = P / (R × T)
  • P = P₀ × (T_atm / T₀)^(g / (R × L))
  • T_atm = T₀ − L × altitude

Engineering Note

Fan performance depends on air density. At lower density (high temperature or altitude), flow rate and pressure reduce. Always apply density correction while selecting fans and blowers.
