Engineering Knowledge

Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction states that the rate of heat transfer through a solid material is directly proportional to the thermal conductivity of the material, the cross-sectional area, and the temperature difference across the material, while being inversely proportional to its thickness.


The governing equation is:

Q = (k × A × ΔT) / L

Symbol Description SI Unit
Q Heat Transfer Rate W
k Thermal Conductivity W/m·K
A Heat Transfer Area m²
ΔT Temperature Difference °C or K
L Material Thickness m

Applications

  • Building insulation design
  • Heat exchanger calculations
  • Boiler and furnace design
  • HVAC engineering
  • Industrial process heating
  • Thermal equipment design
  • Electronic equipment cooling

Engineering Notes

  • Higher thermal conductivity results in greater heat transfer.
  • Increasing insulation thickness reduces heat loss.
  • A larger temperature difference increases heat transfer rate.
  • Copper and aluminum are excellent heat conductors.
  • Insulation materials have very low thermal conductivity.
