Heat Transfer

Calculate heat transfer using Fourier's Law of Conduction

Last Updated: July 15, 2026

Engineering Knowledge

Heat conduction is the transfer of thermal energy through a solid material due to a temperature difference.

According to Fourier’s Law of Heat Conduction, heat transfer rate is:

Q = (k × A × ΔT) / L

Where L is the conduction length (thickness in meters). In this calculator, thickness is entered in mm and converted to meters internally.

Symbol Description Unit
QHeat Transfer RateW
kThermal ConductivityW/m·K
AHeat Transfer Aream²
ΔTTemperature Difference°C or K
LConduction Length (Thickness)m

Engineering Insight

  • Higher thermal conductivity increases heat transfer.
  • Greater area increases heat flow.
  • Higher temperature difference increases heat transfer rate.
  • Greater thickness reduces heat transfer significantly.
