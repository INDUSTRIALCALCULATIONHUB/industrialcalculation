Heat Transfer

Calculate heat transfer using Fourier's Law of Conduction.

Engineering Knowledge

Heat transfer through a solid material occurs due to temperature difference. According to Fourier’s Law, heat flows from higher temperature to lower temperature and depends on material properties and geometry.

Fourier’s Law:

Q = (k × A × ΔT) / L

  • Higher thermal conductivity increases heat transfer
  • Greater thickness reduces heat flow
  • Larger temperature difference increases heat transfer rate
  • Metals like copper conduct heat efficiently
