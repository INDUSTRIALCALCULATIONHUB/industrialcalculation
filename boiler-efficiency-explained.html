Boiler Efficiency

Boiler efficiency is a key performance parameter in thermal engineering that indicates how effectively a boiler converts fuel energy into useful steam energy. It directly impacts fuel consumption, operating cost, and emission levels in industrial systems.

Theory

Boiler efficiency is defined as the ratio of useful heat absorbed by water/steam to the heat released by fuel combustion. It reflects the overall thermal performance of the boiler system.

Basic Definition

  • Efficiency (%) = (Useful Heat Output / Heat Input) × 100

Heat Terms

  • Heat Output → Energy in generated steam
  • Heat Input → Fuel calorific value × fuel consumption

Methods of Efficiency Calculation

1. Direct Method (Input-Output Method)

  • Based on steam generation and fuel consumption
  • Simple and widely used in industry
  • Does not identify individual losses

Efficiency = (Steam Output × Enthalpy) / (Fuel Input × GCV)

2. Indirect Method (Heat Loss Method)

  • Based on detailed heat loss analysis
  • More accurate and diagnostic
  • Used for performance testing and audits

Efficiency = 100 − (Sum of Heat Losses)

Major Boiler Losses

  • Dry flue gas loss – major loss due to high exhaust temperature
  • Moisture loss – due to hydrogen in fuel and ambient moisture
  • Unburnt fuel loss – incomplete combustion
  • Radiation & convection loss – heat loss from boiler surface
  • Blowdown loss – removal of dissolved solids
  • Excess air loss – additional air increases heat carried by flue gas

Factors Affecting Boiler Efficiency

  • Fuel quality and calorific value
  • Excess air control and combustion efficiency
  • Heat transfer surface cleanliness (fouling/soot)
  • Flue gas exit temperature
  • Load variation and part-load operation
  • Boiler insulation and maintenance condition

Engineering Applications

1. Power Plants

  • Steam generation for turbines
  • Efficiency directly affects plant heat rate

2. Industrial Boilers

  • Process steam in chemical, refinery, and textile plants
  • Fuel cost optimization

3. Combustion System Design

  • FD Fan (Forced Draft) – air supply for combustion
  • ID Fan (Induced Draft) – flue gas evacuation
  • Air-fuel ratio optimization

4. Air Pollution Control Systems

  • ESP, bag filter, and scrubber performance linked to flue gas conditions
  • Temperature and flow rate depend on boiler efficiency

5. Heat Recovery Systems

  • Economizer – recovers heat from flue gas
  • Air preheater – improves combustion efficiency
  • Waste heat recovery systems

6. Cooling Towers & Utilities

  • Overall plant efficiency linked to steam cycle performance

Engineering Note

Improving boiler efficiency even by 1–2% can result in significant fuel savings in large industrial plants. Key improvement measures include:

  • Optimizing excess air using O₂ analyzers
  • Reducing flue gas temperature via economizers
  • Regular soot blowing and maintenance
  • Minimizing blowdown losses
