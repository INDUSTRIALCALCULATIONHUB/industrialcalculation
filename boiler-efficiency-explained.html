Boiler Efficiency

Boiler efficiency indicates how effectively a boiler converts fuel energy into useful steam energy. It directly impacts fuel consumption, operating cost, and overall plant performance.

Definition

Boiler efficiency is the ratio of useful heat output in steam to the heat input from fuel.

  • Efficiency (%) = (Heat Output / Heat Input) × 100

Calculation Methods

Direct Method

  • Based on steam generation and fuel consumption
  • Used for routine performance monitoring

Indirect Method

  • Based on heat loss evaluation
  • Used for detailed performance analysis

Major Losses

  • Flue gas heat loss (major loss)
  • Moisture and combustion loss
  • Radiation and convection loss
  • Blowdown loss

Key Influencing Factors

  • Excess air level
  • Flue gas temperature
  • Fuel quality
  • Heat transfer surface condition
  • Boiler load operation

Engineering Relevance

  • FD Fan supplies combustion air
  • ID Fan maintains draft and removes flue gas
  • Efficiency affects flue gas temperature and volume
  • Direct impact on APC systems like ESP and bag filters
  • Improvement reduces fuel cost and emissions

Improvement Practices

  • Optimize excess air
  • Maintain clean heat transfer surfaces
  • Reduce flue gas temperature
  • Control blowdown
