Flange Weight Calculator

Calculate the weight of circular and rectangular flanges using inside dimensions, flange width, thickness, material density and quantity. This calculator is useful for HVAC ducting, industrial piping, fabrication, material estimation, procurement and bill of material (BOM) preparation.

Last Updated: July 14, 2026

Engineering Knowledge

A flange is a circular ring or rectangular frame used to connect ducts, pipes, equipment and fabricated structures. Flanges provide a rigid bolted or welded connection while maintaining dimensional accuracy during installation. The weight of a flange depends on its centre line length, cross-sectional area, material density and quantity.

This calculator determines the flange centre line length from the entered dimensions. It then calculates the cross-sectional area using the flange width and thickness. The flange volume is obtained by multiplying the cross-sectional area by the centre line length, and the weight is calculated by multiplying the volume by the selected material density.

Formula

Cross-sectional Area = Flange Width × Flange Thickness

Volume = Cross-sectional Area × Centre Line Length

Weight = Volume × Material Density

Rectangular Flange Calculation

Parameter Formula
Outside Length Inside Length + 2 × Flange Width
Outside Width Inside Width + 2 × Flange Width
Centre Line Length 2 × [(Inside Length + Flange Width) + (Inside Width + Flange Width)]

Circular Flange Calculation

Parameter Formula
Centre Line Diameter Inside Diameter + Flange Width
Centre Line Length π × (Inside Diameter + Flange Width)

Effect of Each Parameter

  • Larger inside dimensions: Increase the flange centre line length and total weight.
  • Greater flange width: Increases both centre line length and cross-sectional area.
  • Greater flange thickness: Produces a heavier flange.
  • Higher material density: Produces a heavier flange for identical dimensions.
  • Higher quantity: Increases the total weight proportionally.
  • Longer centre line length: Requires more material and increases the flange weight.

  • Weight is calculated using the flange centre line method.

  • All dimensions are entered in millimetres (mm).

  • Results represent theoretical flange weight and do not include manufacturing tolerances.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is flange weight calculated?

The calculator determines the centre line length, calculates the cross-sectional area from the flange width and thickness, determines the flange volume, and multiplies it by the selected material density to calculate the flange weight.

Can this calculator calculate both circular and rectangular flanges?

Yes. The calculator supports both circular and rectangular flanges. Simply select the required flange type and enter the corresponding dimensions.

Are all dimensions entered in millimetres?

Yes. All dimensions, including inside length, inside width, inside diameter, flange width and flange thickness, must be entered in millimetres (mm).

Which materials are supported?

The calculator supports Mild Steel, Carbon Steel, IS 2062 Structural Steel, Stainless Steel 304, Stainless Steel 316, Stainless Steel 316L, Stainless Steel 409, Aluminium, Cast Iron, Copper, Brass, Bronze, Titanium and Nickel.

Are bolt holes and welds included in the weight?

No. The calculated weight is based only on the flange geometry. Bolt holes, slots, welds, bolts, nuts and gaskets are not included in the calculation.

Can I calculate stainless steel flange weight?

Yes. Select the required stainless steel grade from the material dropdown, and the calculator automatically uses the corresponding material density.

Is the calculated weight theoretical or actual?

The result is the theoretical flange weight based on the entered dimensions and typical material density. Manufacturing tolerances, machining and fabrication allowances are not included.

Can this calculator be used for HVAC duct flanges?

Yes. It is suitable for HVAC duct flanges, industrial ventilation systems, bag filter ductwork, industrial piping systems and general fabrication applications.

Does the calculator calculate the total weight for multiple flanges?

Yes. Enter the required quantity, and the calculator automatically calculates the total weight for all flanges.

Can I use this calculator for fabrication cost estimation?

Yes. The calculated flange weight can be used for estimating material quantities, preparing bills of materials (BOMs), procurement planning, transportation studies and preliminary fabrication cost estimation.

What is the centre line method?

The centre line method calculates the flange length along the centre of its cross-section. This provides an accurate estimate of material volume and flange weight and is widely used for fabrication and estimation.
