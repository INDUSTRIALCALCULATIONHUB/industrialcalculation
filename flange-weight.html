Flange Weight

Calculate flange weight using dimensions and material density

Engineering Knowledge

A flange is a mechanical component used to connect pipes, valves, pumps, pressure vessels, and other equipment. Accurate flange weight calculation is important for fabrication, material estimation, transportation, structural support design, and lifting operations.

The calculator determines the flange weight by calculating the net volume of the flange (after subtracting bolt holes) and multiplying it by the selected material density.

Weight = Net Volume × Material Density

The net volume is obtained from the annular flange area minus the total bolt hole area, multiplied by the flange thickness.

Calculation Formula

Formula Description
A = π/4 × (OD² − ID²) Gross flange area
Ah = n × π/4 × d² Total bolt hole area
Anet = A − Ah Net flange area
V = Anet × t Net flange volume
W = V × ρ Flange weight

Variables Used

Symbol Description Unit
OD Outer Diameter mm
ID Inner Diameter mm
t Flange Thickness mm
n Number of Bolt Holes —
d Bolt Hole Diameter mm
ρ Material Density kg/m³
V Volume m³
W Weight kg

Typical Material Densities

Material Density (kg/m³)
Carbon Steel 7850
Stainless Steel 304 8000
Stainless Steel 316 7990
Cast Iron 7140
Aluminium 2700
Copper 8900

Engineering Notes

  • All dimensions are entered in millimetres and automatically converted to metres for calculation.
  • The weight calculation subtracts the material removed by bolt holes.
  • This calculator assumes a flat flange with uniform thickness.
  • Raised face, hub, weld neck and other flange features are not included.
  • For forged flanges, actual manufacturer weight may differ slightly due to machining allowances and tolerances.
  • Always verify final flange weights using fabrication drawings or applicable standards such as ASME B16.5, ASME B16.47 or EN 1092 when required.
