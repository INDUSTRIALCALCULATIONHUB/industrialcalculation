Flange Weight

Calculate the weight of circular and rectangular flange

Engineering Knowledge

A flange is a ring or rectangular frame used to join ducts, pipes, equipment and fabricated structures. The weight of a flange depends upon its developed centre line length, flange cross-sectional area, material density and quantity.

The calculator first determines the centre line length of the flange. It then calculates the cross-sectional area from flange width and thickness. The volume is obtained by multiplying the area with the centre line length. Finally, the flange weight is obtained by multiplying the volume by the material density.

Formula

Volume = Cross Section Area × Centre Line Length

Weight = Volume × Density

Rectangular Flange

Parameter Formula
Outside Length Inside Length + 2 × Flange Width
Outside Width Inside Width + 2 × Flange Width
Centre Line Length 2 × [(Inside Length + Flange Width) + (Inside Width + Flange Width)]

Circular Flange

Parameter Formula
Centre Line Diameter Inside Diameter + Flange Width
Centre Line Length π × (Inside Diameter + Flange Width)

Typical Material Density

Material Density (kg/m³)
Mild Steel 7850
Galvanized Steel 7850
Stainless Steel 304 8000
Stainless Steel 316 8000
Aluminium 2700

Effect of Each Parameter

  • Larger inside dimensions: Increase the flange centre line length and total weight.
  • Greater flange width: Increases both centre line length and cross-sectional area.
  • Greater flange thickness: Produces a heavier flange.
  • Higher material density: Produces a heavier flange for identical dimensions.
  • Higher quantity: Increases the total weight proportionally.

Engineering Applications

  • HVAC duct flanges
  • Industrial ventilation
  • Dust collection systems
  • Bag filter ducting
  • Equipment fabrication
  • Structural fabrication
  • BOM preparation
  • Procurement estimation
  • Transportation planning
  • Lifting calculations

Engineering Notes

  • Weight is calculated using the flange centre line.
  • Corner welds, holes, bolts, nuts and gaskets are not included.
  • All dimensions are entered in millimetres.
  • Material density is selected from the dropdown list.
  • The calculator is intended for fabrication estimation and engineering calculations.
