Pressure Vessel Design Basics

Understanding stress and design principles in industrial pressure systems

⚙️ What is a Pressure Vessel?

A pressure vessel is a container designed to hold gases or liquids at a pressure substantially different from the ambient pressure. They are widely used in chemical plants, refineries, and power industries.

📐 Key Design Stresses

  • Hoop Stress: Circumferential stress in the vessel wall
  • Longitudinal Stress: Stress along the length of the vessel
  • Radial Stress: Stress acting inward/outward (usually small)

📊 Basic Formulas

Hoop Stress:

σh = (P × r) / t

Longitudinal Stress:

σl = (P × r) / (2t)

  • P = Internal pressure
  • r = Internal radius
  • t = Wall thickness

🏭 Materials Used

  • Carbon steel
  • Stainless steel
  • Alloy steel
  • Composite materials (advanced applications)

⚠️ Safety Factors

Pressure vessels must be designed with safety factors to avoid failure due to overpressure, corrosion, or fatigue.

📊 Industrial Applications

  • Boilers and steam systems
  • Oil & gas separators
  • Chemical reactors
  • Compressed air tanks
