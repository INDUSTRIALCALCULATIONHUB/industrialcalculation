⚙️ Pressure Vessel Design Basics

Engineering stress concepts used in industrial pressure systems

⚙️ What is a Pressure Vessel?

A pressure vessel is a container designed to hold gases or liquids at a pressure substantially different from ambient pressure. They are widely used in chemical plants, refineries, and power industries.

📐 Key Design Stresses

  • Hoop Stress: Circumferential stress in vessel wall
  • Longitudinal Stress: Stress along vessel length
  • Radial Stress: Usually small compared to other stresses

📊 Basic Formulas

Hoop Stress: σh = (P × r) / t
Longitudinal Stress: σl = (P × r) / (2t)
  • P = Internal pressure
  • r = Internal radius
  • t = Wall thickness

🏭 Materials Used

  • Carbon steel
  • Stainless steel
  • Alloy steel
  • Composite materials

⚠️ Safety Considerations

Design includes safety factors for pressure surges, corrosion allowance, and fatigue life to prevent catastrophic failure.

📊 Applications

  • Boilers and steam systems
  • Oil & gas separators
  • Chemical reactors
  • Compressed air tanks

📘 Theory / Information / Notes

Pressure vessel design follows thin and thick cylinder theory depending on thickness ratio. Proper stress distribution ensures safe operation under high internal pressure conditions. Material selection and corrosion allowance are critical for long-term reliability.
