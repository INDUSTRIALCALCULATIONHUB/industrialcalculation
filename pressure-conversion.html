Engineering Knowledge

  • All pressure conversions are referenced to the SI unit Pascal (Pa), defined as 1 N/m²
  • 1 bar = 100,000 Pa (commonly used in process industries and hydraulic systems)
  • 1 atmosphere (atm) = 101,325 Pa (standard atmospheric pressure at sea level)
  • 1 psi (pound-force per square inch) = 6,894.76 Pa (widely used in mechanical and pipeline engineering)
  • 1 mmWC (millimeter water column) = 9.80665 Pa (used in HVAC, ventilation, and low-pressure measurement systems)
  • Pressure measurement depends on reference condition: absolute pressure vs gauge pressure must be clearly distinguished in engineering applications
  • Hydraulic and fluid systems design requires pressure unit consistency to avoid conversion errors in force and flow calculations
  • High-pressure systems require consideration of material strength, safety factor, and pressure rating standards (ASME, ISO)
