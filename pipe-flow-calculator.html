Engineering Knowledge

Pipe flow calculations are fundamental in fluid mechanics and are used to determine the relationship between pipe diameter, flow rate and fluid velocity. Proper velocity selection helps minimize pressure losses, erosion and energy consumption.

Fundamental Equations

  • Pipe Area (A) = πD² / 4
  • Flow Rate (Q) = A × V
  • Velocity (V) = Q / A
  • Diameter (D) = √(4Q / πV)

Engineering Considerations

  • Water systems typically operate between 1–3 m/s.
  • High velocities increase pressure drop and pumping power.
  • Very low velocities may cause sediment deposition.
  • Larger pipe diameters reduce friction losses.
  • Pipe roughness affects actual pressure loss.
  • Always verify velocity against project specifications.
