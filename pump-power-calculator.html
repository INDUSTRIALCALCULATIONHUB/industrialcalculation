Pump Motor Selection

Calculate hydraulic power and select suitable motor size

Engineering Knowledge

Pump power is the energy required to move fluid against a specified head at a given flow rate. Motor selection should consider hydraulic power, pump efficiency, service factor, starting torque, and future operating margins.

Fundamental Formula

  • Hydraulic Power (kW) = (ρ × g × Q × H) ÷ 1000
  • For water: Hydraulic Power (kW) = 9.81 × Q × H (Q in m³/s)
  • For flow in m³/hr: Hydraulic Power (kW) = 0.002725 × Q × H
  • ρ = Fluid density (kg/m³)
  • g = 9.81 m/s²
  • Q = Flow rate
  • H = Total Dynamic Head (m)

Engineering Considerations

  • Typical pump efficiency: 60–85%.
  • Typical motor efficiency: 88–96%.
  • Typical design margin for motor selection: 10–15%.
  • Pump efficiency significantly affects operating power consumption.
  • Oversized motors reduce efficiency at partial loads.
  • High head applications require careful NPSH and cavitation evaluation.

Industrial Applications

  • Water treatment plants
  • Boiler feed systems
  • Cooling water circulation
  • Chemical process pumping
  • HVAC chilled water systems
