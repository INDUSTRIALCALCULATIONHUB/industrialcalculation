Engineering Knowledge

Temperature conversion is based on thermodynamic scale relationships used in engineering systems to ensure consistency between empirical and absolute temperature measurements.

  • Celsius (°C) is a relative temperature scale based on the freezing and boiling points of water at standard atmospheric pressure
  • Kelvin (K) is the absolute thermodynamic temperature scale and is the SI base unit for temperature
  • Fahrenheit (°F) is a historical empirical scale still used in legacy systems and certain industrial standards
  • In thermodynamic calculations, Kelvin must be used to ensure valid energy and gas law computations
  • Temperature differences (ΔT) are identical in Celsius and Kelvin but differ in Fahrenheit scaling
  • Incorrect scale selection can lead to major errors in heat transfer, energy balance, and process design calculations

Engineering Applications

  • Thermodynamics and heat transfer analysis
  • HVAC and refrigeration system design
  • Chemical process and reaction engineering
  • Power plant and energy system modeling
  • Material behavior under thermal stress conditions

Theory

  • Celsius is based on water freezing/boiling points
  • Kelvin is absolute thermodynamic scale
  • Fahrenheit is used in legacy systems
  • Always use Kelvin for thermodynamic equations
