Duct Weight

Calculate the sheet metal surface area, weight per metre, and total weight of rectangular and circular ducts using dimensions in millimetres. Suitable for HVAC, industrial ventilation, dust collection, and process ductwork.

Last Updated: July 13, 2026

Engineering Knowledge

Duct weight is determined from the sheet metal surface area, material thickness, and material density. The calculated weight is important for HVAC design, duct supports, structural loading, transportation, fabrication, and installation planning.

For straight ducts, the sheet metal surface area is calculated from the duct perimeter and length. The sheet volume is then obtained by multiplying the surface area by the sheet thickness. Finally, the duct weight is calculated by multiplying the sheet volume by the material density.

Formula:

Surface Area = Perimeter × Length

Volume = Surface Area × Thickness

Weight = Volume × Material Density

Rectangular Duct

Parameter Formula
Perimeter 2 × (Width + Height)
Surface Area 2 × (W + H) × L

Circular Duct

Parameter Formula
Circumference π × Diameter
Surface Area π × D × L

Worked Example

Calculate the weight of a rectangular galvanized steel duct having:

  • Width = 1500 mm
  • Height = 1200 mm
  • Length = 2500 mm
  • Thickness = 3 mm
  • Density = 7850 kg/m³

Perimeter = 2 × (1.5 + 1.2) = 5.4 m

Surface Area = 5.4 × 2.5 = 13.5 m²

Volume = 13.5 × 0.003 = 0.0405 m³

Weight = 0.0405 × 7850 = 317.93 kg

Aluminium 2700

Effect of Each Parameter

  • Larger duct dimensions: Increase the sheet metal surface area and total weight.
  • Greater duct length: Increases both surface area and total material requirement.
  • Higher sheet thickness: Produces proportionally heavier ducts.
  • Material density: Stainless steel ducts are heavier than aluminium ducts of identical dimensions.

Design Considerations

  • Select sheet thickness according to duct pressure class.
  • Large ducts generally require stiffeners and reinforcement angles.
  • Support spacing should consider the total dead load of the duct system.
  • Additional weight due to insulation and cladding should be included during structural design.
  • Use corrosion-resistant materials where required by the operating environment.

Applicable Standards

Duct fabrication is commonly carried out according to SMACNA, DW/144, IS 655, or project-specific specifications. This calculator estimates sheet weight only and should not replace detailed fabrication drawings or structural design calculations.

Engineering Notes

  • This calculator estimates the weight of straight duct sections only.
  • Flanges, stiffeners, reinforcement angles, access doors, dampers, insulation, and accessories are not included.
  • Enter all duct dimensions, including width, height, diameter, length, and plate or sheet thickness, in millimetres (mm). The calculator automatically converts these values internally for accurate calculations.
  • For insulated ducts, calculate insulation weight separately and add it to the sheet weight.
  • Use project specifications or applicable standards for final fabrication and structural design.
  • Actual fabricated duct weight may increase due to flanges, reinforcement angles, stiffeners, and access doors.
  • Large industrial ducts generally require reinforcement according to project specifications.
  • Material density may vary slightly depending on alloy composition and manufacturing process.
  • This calculator is intended for preliminary engineering, fabrication estimation, and material quantity calculations.
