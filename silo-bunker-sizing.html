Engineering Knowledge

What is a Silo?

A silo is a vertical storage structure used in industries such as cement, power plants, steel plants, and agriculture for storing bulk materials like cement, fly ash, coal, grain, and powders. Silos are generally circular in shape to improve structural strength and material flow.

What is a Bunker?

A bunker is a rectangular storage structure used for bulk material storage and handling. It is commonly used in power plants and industrial plants for coal, limestone, and raw material storage. Bunkers typically have a hopper bottom to assist controlled discharge of material.

Difference Between Silo and Bunker

Silo Bunker
Circular geometry Rectangular geometry
Better structural strength Simple construction

What is Angle of Repose?

Angle of repose is the maximum angle at which bulk material remains stable without sliding. It directly affects usable storage volume inside silos and bunkers. A higher angle of repose reduces effective storage volume.

  • Cement: 30° – 35°
  • Fly Ash: 25° – 30°
  • Coal: 35° – 45°
  • Limestone: 35° – 40°
  • Sand: 30° – 40°

Engineering Formulas Used

Component Formula
Cylinder Volume V = π × R² × H
Cone / Hopper Volume V = (1/3) × π × H × (R² + Rr + r²)
Rectangular Hopper V = (1/3) × L × W × H
Effective Volume Veff = Total Volume − Material Cone Volume

Design Considerations

  • Hopper angle must be sufficient for smooth material flow
  • Angle of repose reduces usable storage volume
  • Material flow behavior affects discharge efficiency
  • Outlet size controls discharge rate
  • Bridging and ratholing must be considered in real design
