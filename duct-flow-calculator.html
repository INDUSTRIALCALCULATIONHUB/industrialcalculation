Duct Flow

Airflow, velocity and duct sizing

Engineering Knowledge

What is Duct Flow Rate?

Duct flow rate is the volume of air passing through a duct per unit time. It is a key parameter in HVAC and ventilation system design. It is usually expressed in m³/s, m³/hr, or CFM.

Basic Formula

FormulaDescription
Q = A × VFlow Rate = Area × Velocity
V = Q / AVelocity = Flow Rate / Area
A = Q / VDuct Area = Flow Rate / Velocity

Typical Air Velocity Ranges

ApplicationVelocity Range
Comfort AC2 – 5 m/s
Commercial HVAC5 – 8 m/s
Industrial Ventilation8 – 15 m/s
Dust Collection15 – 25 m/s
Pneumatic Transport20 – 35 m/s

Engineering Notes

  • Higher velocity reduces duct size but increases fan power.
  • Lower velocity reduces noise and pressure loss.
  • Circular ducts have lower friction losses than rectangular ducts.
  • Proper sizing avoids energy wastage in HVAC systems.
  • Always balance airflow, noise, and pressure drop in design.
