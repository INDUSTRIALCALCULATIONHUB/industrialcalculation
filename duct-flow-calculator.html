What is Duct Flow Rate?

Duct flow rate is the volume of air passing through a duct in a given time. It is commonly expressed in m³/s, m³/hr, or CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute). Correct airflow is essential for HVAC systems, industrial ventilation, dust collection systems, and process air applications.

Basic Duct Flow Equation

Formula Description
Q = A × V Flow Rate = Cross-sectional Area × Air Velocity
V = Q / A Velocity from Flow Rate
A = Q / V Required Duct Area

Typical Air Velocity Recommendations

Application Recommended Velocity
Comfort Air Conditioning 2–5 m/s
Commercial HVAC 5–8 m/s
Industrial Ventilation 8–15 m/s
Dust Collection 15–25 m/s
Pneumatic Conveying 20–35 m/s

Engineering Notes

  • Lower air velocity reduces pressure loss and operating noise.
  • Higher velocity decreases duct size but increases fan power consumption.
  • Circular ducts generally have lower pressure losses than rectangular ducts.
  • Maintain uniform airflow distribution throughout the duct network.
  • Select duct dimensions based on both airflow requirement and acceptable pressure drop.
