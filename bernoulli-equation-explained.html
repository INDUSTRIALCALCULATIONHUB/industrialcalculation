Bernoulli Equation

The Bernoulli equation represents conservation of mechanical energy in a flowing fluid. It relates pressure, velocity, and elevation, and is widely used in analysis of pipelines, ducts, and pumping systems.

Equation

  • P/ρg + V²/2g + z = constant
  • P → Pressure (Pa)
  • ρ → Fluid density (kg/m³)
  • V → Velocity (m/s)
  • g → Gravity (9.81 m/s²)
  • z → Elevation (m)

Physical Meaning

  • Increase in velocity → decrease in pressure
  • Decrease in velocity → increase in pressure
  • Total energy along a streamline remains constant

Assumptions

  • Steady flow
  • Incompressible fluid
  • Negligible friction losses
  • Flow along a streamline

Engineering Relevance

  • Pump head calculation in piping systems
  • Pressure and velocity analysis in ducts
  • Flow measurement using Venturi and orifice meters
  • Fan system pressure distribution (ID / FD systems)
  • Basis for pressure drop and energy balance calculations

Practical Form (With Losses)

  • P₁/ρg + V₁²/2g + z₁ + Hp = P₂/ρg + V₂²/2g + z₂ + Hf
  • Hp → Pump head added
  • Hf → Head loss due to friction
