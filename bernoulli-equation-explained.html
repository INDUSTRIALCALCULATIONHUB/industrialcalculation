Bernoulli Equation

The Bernoulli equation is a fundamental principle of fluid mechanics that represents conservation of mechanical energy in a flowing fluid. It establishes the relationship between pressure, velocity, and elevation in steady flow systems.

Theory

For an incompressible, frictionless flow along a streamline, the total energy remains constant. This energy consists of pressure energy, kinetic energy, and potential energy.

Bernoulli Equation

  • P/ρg + V²/2g + z = constant

Terms Explanation

  • P/ρg → Pressure head
  • V²/2g → Velocity head
  • z → Elevation head
  • Total = Constant total head

SI Form (Energy Form)

  • P + (½ρV²) + (ρgz) = constant

Assumptions

  • Steady flow
  • Incompressible fluid
  • Negligible viscosity (no friction loss)
  • Flow along a streamline
  • No shaft work or heat transfer

Physical Interpretation

An increase in fluid velocity results in a decrease in pressure, and vice versa. This principle explains flow acceleration in converging sections and pressure recovery in diffusers.

  • High velocity → Low pressure
  • Low velocity → High pressure

Engineering Applications

1. Pump and Piping Systems

  • Total head calculation for pump selection
  • Suction and discharge pressure analysis
  • Determination of pump NPSH requirements

2. Fan and Air Handling Systems

  • Velocity pressure and static pressure relationship
  • Duct design and airflow balancing
  • ID Fan / FD Fan system pressure analysis
  • Axial and centrifugal fan performance evaluation

3. Flow Measurement Devices

  • Venturi meter
  • Orifice meter
  • Pitot tube (velocity measurement)

4. HVAC and Duct Systems

  • Air velocity and pressure distribution
  • Duct sizing and pressure drop calculations

5. Cooling Towers and Process Systems

  • Air and water flow energy balance
  • Pressure drop across packing and nozzles

6. Air Pollution Control Systems

  • Gas flow behavior in ducts and stacks
  • Pressure distribution across ESP, bag filters, scrubbers

7. Aerospace and Aerodynamics

  • Lift generation on aircraft wings
  • Flow over airfoils

Extended Bernoulli Equation (Real Systems)

For practical engineering systems, losses and external work must be included:

  • P₁/ρg + V₁²/2g + z₁ + Hp = P₂/ρg + V₂²/2g + z₂ + Hf
  • Hp → Pump head added
  • Hf → Head loss due to friction

Engineering Note

In real industrial systems, friction losses, turbulence, fittings, bends, and equipment significantly affect energy distribution. Therefore, Bernoulli equation is always used along with head loss calculations (Darcy-Weisbach or empirical methods).
