What is Bernoulli Equation?

Fundamental Principle of Fluid Mechanics

📌 Introduction

The Bernoulli equation is one of the most important principles in fluid mechanics. It describes the relationship between pressure, velocity, and height of a flowing fluid.

📊 Bernoulli Equation

P/ρg + V²/2g + h = constant

  • P = Pressure energy
  • ρ = Fluid density
  • V = Flow velocity
  • g = Gravity (9.81 m/s²)
  • h = Height elevation

🌊 Physical Meaning

Bernoulli’s equation is based on the law of conservation of energy. When fluid speed increases, pressure decreases and vice versa.

🏭 Engineering Applications

  • Pump and turbine design
  • Pipe flow systems
  • Aircraft wing lift generation
  • Water distribution systems
