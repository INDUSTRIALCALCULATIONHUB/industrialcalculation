What is Bernoulli Equation?

Fundamental principle of fluid mechanics used to relate pressure, velocity, and elevation energy in flowing fluids.

Introduction

The Bernoulli equation is one of the most important principles in fluid mechanics. It describes energy conservation in flowing fluids.

Bernoulli Equation

P/ρg + V²/2g + h = constant

  • P = Pressure energy
  • ρ = Fluid density
  • V = Velocity
  • g = Gravity (9.81 m/s²)
  • h = Height

Physical Meaning

When fluid velocity increases, pressure decreases. This is fundamental in pump and pipeline systems.

Engineering Applications

  • Pump and turbine design
  • Pipe flow systems
  • Aircraft wing lift
  • Water distribution networks
